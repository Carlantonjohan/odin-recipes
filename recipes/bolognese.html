Classic Bolognese Sauce Recipe

Bolognese sauce, or ragù alla Bolognese, is a rich and hearty Italian meat sauce that transforms simple ingredients into a flavorful dish. This sauce is characterized by its slow cooking process, which melds the flavors of meat, vegetables, and tomatoes into a velvety sauce, perfect for serving over pasta.

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 1 carrot, finely diced
  • 1 celery rib, finely diced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 pound ground beef
  • ½ pound ground pork
  • 1 ¼ cups dry red wine
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 28 ounces whole tomatoes with juice
  • 4 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon Italian seasoning
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Pasta of choice (e.g., pappardelle or tagliatelle)
  • Grated Parmesan cheese for serving

Instructions

  1. In a large pot, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion and cook until softened, about 3-4 minutes.
  2. Add the garlic, carrot, and celery, cooking until they are tender, about 5 minutes.
  3. Stir in the ground beef and pork, breaking it up with a wooden spoon until browned. Drain excess fat.
  4. Pour in the red wine and let it simmer uncovered until it evaporates, about 10 minutes.
  5. Add the milk and simmer until it has evaporated, about 8 minutes.
  6. Stir in the tomatoes (with juice), tomato paste, Italian seasoning, and bay leaf. Break up the tomatoes with a spoon.
  7. Cover and simmer for 30 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sauce thickens. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  8. Cook the pasta according to package instructions. Drain, reserving some pasta water.
  9. Toss the pasta with the Bolognese sauce, adding reserved pasta water to reach desired consistency.
  10. Serve hot, topped with grated Parmesan cheese.
